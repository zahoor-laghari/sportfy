Our History

Polo is one of the world’s oldest known team sports, originating in ancient Persia over 2,000 years ago. It began as a training game for cavalry units and evolved into a royal pastime. Over centuries, it spread to India, China, and beyond.

The modern version of the game was shaped in British India during the 19th century, and today, polo is a global sport known for its speed, precision, and elegance, played on horseback with four players per team.

Popular Formats of Polo

Field Polo

The most traditional form played on a large grass field with four players per team.

Arena Polo

Played indoors or on smaller outdoor fields with three players per team; faster-paced with boards around the arena.

Snow Polo

A winter variant played on snow-covered fields, often in luxurious alpine destinations like St. Moritz.

Interesting Facts About Polo

  • Polo is one of the oldest known team sports, with origins over 2,000 years ago in ancient Persia.
  • It was used as cavalry training for warriors and sometimes involved over 100 players per side.
  • The modern version of polo was developed in India and formalized by the British in the 19th century.
  • Polo is often referred to as the "Sport of Kings" and has been played by royalty around the world.
  • There are variations of polo played on snow, camels, and even elephants in some countries.
