Our History

Javelin throw is a track and field event where athletes compete to throw a spear-like implement called a javelin as far as possible. Originating from ancient hunting and warfare techniques, it has become a popular sport worldwide, especially in athletics competitions such as the Olympics and World Championships.

The event combines strength, speed, and technique, requiring athletes to run within a designated runway before launching the javelin into a marked sector. The thrower who achieves the longest valid throw wins the competition, highlighting the skillful balance between power and precision.

Three Major Formats of Game

Traditional Javelin Throw

The classic format where athletes get a set number of attempts to throw the javelin as far as possible, usually during track and field meets.

Indoor Javelin Throw

A variation held indoors with a shorter runway and modified equipment, focusing on technique and precision in controlled conditions.

Para Javelin Throw

A competitive format for athletes with disabilities, featuring classifications to ensure fair competition and showcasing remarkable skill and determination.

Interesting Facts About JavelinThrow

  • Javelin throw originated in ancient Greece as a part of the Olympic Games.
  • Modern javelin throw was introduced in the Olympics in 1908 for men and in 1932 for women.
  • Neeraj Chopra became the first Indian to win an Olympic gold medal in athletics in 2021.
  • The men's javelin weighs 800 grams and must be at least 2.6 meters long.
  • Uwe Hohn is the only athlete to throw the javelin over 100 meters (104.80 m in 1984), leading to a redesign of the javelin.
