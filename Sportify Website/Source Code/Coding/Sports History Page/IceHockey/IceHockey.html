Our History

Ice hockey is a fast-paced team sport played on ice. Originating in Canada in the 19th century, it is especially popular in countries like Canada, the USA, Russia, Sweden, and Finland.

Played with six players per team including a goaltender, the aim is to score goals with a puck using sticks. Known for speed and intensity, ice hockey has grown into one of the most exciting winter sports globally.

Three Major Formats of Game

National Hockey League (NHL)

The top professional league, mainly in the US and Canada. Teams compete in a long season followed by playoffs for the Stanley Cup.

International Ice Hockey Federation (IIHF)

Governs events like the World Championship and Olympics, featuring national teams from across the globe.

Winter Olympics

Every four years, national teams compete for gold. It’s one of the most viewed and awaited ice hockey tournaments worldwide.

Interesting Facts About Ice Hockey

  • The Stanley Cup is over 130 years old and passed to each year’s winning team.
  • Wayne Gretzky holds more assists than anyone else has total points.
  • Pucks can travel faster than 100 mph.
  • The NHL was founded in 1917 with just 4 teams.
  • Ice Hockey was added to the Olympics in 1924.
