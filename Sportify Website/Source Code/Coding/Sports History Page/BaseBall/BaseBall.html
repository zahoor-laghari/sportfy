Our History

Baseball is a bat-and-ball game that originated in the United States in the 18th century. The modern form of the game evolved in the mid-19th century and became the national pastime of America.

Played between two teams of nine players each, the goal is to score runs by hitting a ball and running through bases arranged in a diamond. The game is rich in tradition, with a major cultural impact across the Americas and East Asia.

Popular Formats of Baseball

Major League Baseball (MLB)

The premier professional league in the USA, featuring top teams like the Yankees and Dodgers.

Nippon Professional Baseball (NPB)

Japan's top baseball league, known for its passionate fans and high-level play.

World Baseball Classic (WBC)

International competition featuring national teams from around the world.

Interesting Facts About Baseball

  • Baseball originated in the United States in the 18th century and became the national pastime by the 19th century.
  • The first officially recorded baseball game took place in 1846 in Hoboken, New Jersey.
  • Major League Baseball (MLB) was established in 1903 and is now one of the most popular sports leagues globally.
  • Babe Ruth, one of the greatest players in history, hit 714 career home runs.
  • The longest professional baseball game lasted 33 innings and over 8 hours of play.
