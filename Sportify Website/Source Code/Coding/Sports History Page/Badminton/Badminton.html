Our History

Badminton originated in British India during the mid-19th century and quickly became a popular sport worldwide. Played using racquets and a shuttlecock, it is a fast-paced game that demands agility, speed, and precision.

Matches are usually played indoors between singles or doubles, and players score by landing the shuttlecock in the opponent's court. Badminton is now a popular Olympic sport and has major followings in countries like China, Indonesia, India, Denmark, and South Korea.

Major Event Categories

Singles

One-on-one matches that require incredible stamina and individual skill.

Doubles

Played with two players per side, demanding coordination and quick reflexes.

Mixed Doubles

Teams composed of one male and one female player per side, testing teamwork and balance.

Interesting Facts About Badminton

  • The fastest recorded badminton smash was 426 km/h by Fu Haifeng.
  • Badminton has been an Olympic sport since 1992.
  • Shuttlecocks are traditionally made from goose feathers.
  • Matches are played up to 21 points per game, best of 3 games.
  • China has won the most Olympic gold medals in badminton.
