Our History

Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport between two teams of eleven players each. It originated in England and is now one of the most popular sports in the world, especially in countries like Pakistan, India, Australia, England, South Africa, and Sri Lanka. The game is known for its spirit, strategy, and the unique balance between batting and bowling skills.

The playing field is oval-shaped with a rectangular 22-yard pitch in the center. Each team takes turns to bat and bowl. The batting team tries to score as many runs as possible while the bowling and fielding side tries to dismiss the batsmen. The team with the most runs at the end of the match wins.

Three Major Formats of Game

Test Cricket

The longest format of the game, played over five days with unlimited overs and two innings per team.

One Day Internationals (ODI)

A 50-over per side format known for its balance between strategy and pace, usually completed in one day.

Twenty20 (T20)

The shortest and most thrilling format, where each team plays 20 overs, emphasizing fast-paced action.

Interesting Facts About Cricket

  • Cricket was first played in the 16th century in England.
  • Sachin Tendulkar is the only player with 100 international centuries.
  • The longest cricket match lasted 12 days.
  • India won its first World Cup in 1983 under Kapil Dev.
  • MS Dhoni is the only captain to win all three ICC trophies.
