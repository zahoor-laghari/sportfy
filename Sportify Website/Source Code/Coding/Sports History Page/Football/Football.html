Our History

Football, known as soccer in some countries, is the world's most popular sport with over 4 billion fans. Originating in England in the 19th century, the modern game has roots in various ball games played throughout history. The sport is governed by FIFA and features competitions like the World Cup, which is the most-watched sporting event globally.

Played between two teams of eleven players, the objective is to score by getting the ball into the opposing team's goal. Players primarily use their feet but can also use other parts of their body except their arms and hands (except for the goalkeeper). The game is known for its fast pace, skillful play, and passionate fan culture.

Major Football Competitions

FIFA World Cup

The premier international tournament held every 4 years, featuring 32 national teams competing for the most prestigious trophy in football.

UEFA Champions League

Europe's elite club competition where top teams from domestic leagues compete for continental glory and the iconic trophy.

Domestic Leagues

National competitions like Premier League, La Liga, and Bundesliga featuring intense rivalries and world-class talent.
