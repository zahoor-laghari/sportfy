Our History

Judo is a modern martial art, combat, and Olympic sport created in Japan in 1882 by Jigoro Kano. It evolved from jujutsu, emphasizing the principle of maximum efficiency with minimal effort. Today, Judo is practiced around the world as a competitive sport and a way to develop physical and mental discipline.

Judo matches take place on mats where two opponents attempt to throw, pin, or force a submission using grappling techniques. The sport emphasizes respect, control, and mutual welfare.

Main Event Categories

Kata

Demonstration of pre-arranged techniques showcasing form and technique.

Shiai

Competitive fighting between judokas in weight and skill classes.

Randori

Free practice involving live sparring under less formal rules than competition.

Interesting Facts About Judo

  • Judo means "gentle way" in Japanese.
  • It became an Olympic sport for men in 1964 and for women in 1992.
  • Jigoro Kano, the founder, was also Japan's first IOC member.
  • There are seven colored belts before reaching black belt.
  • Judo emphasizes using the opponent's force against them.
